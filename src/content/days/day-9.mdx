Woke up to some rain. Well, *lots* of rain. The caravan leaders were going to serve a pancake breakfast — cancelled. 😞

Keith headed out to wash the truck; I went to see a little of the nearby town, York. It helped us confirm we were on the right road…

Shopped a little — a mix of old and new, and many temptations for Nana. Walked away with something Keith picked out for the little one, some flavor packets for my morning coffee, and a cobbler mix Keith found for one of our potlucks. I browsed the consignment and antique section but walked away empty-handed.

![Inside the York shop — antiques and new goods mixed together](/images/day-9/IMG_4576.jpeg)

Three fellows were working on putting a metal roof on one of the local shops. I think the one balancing on the angled section lost the rock-paper-scissors contest…

![Roofers working on an angled metal roof in downtown York](/images/day-9/IMG_4575.jpeg)

Finally found a Post Office open to send off some postcards!

![York, NE Post Office](/images/day-9/IMG_4580.jpeg)

Reminds me of the old building in Elkin, NC. This downtown was being taken care of — people were shopping and the buildings are maintained.

![Well-kept downtown York street scene](/images/day-9/IMG_4578-2.jpeg)

This is the picture where I discovered why you'd use the **0.5 setting** on the Apple camera — I could finally get the entire mural in frame.

## Laundromat Social Hour

Got back to the campground in time to do a couple loads of laundry. Loved that they had a change machine — I put in some extra dollars for future quarters. When I arrived at the laundromat, I found Marsha finishing her loads. We chatted through mine. Like it was choreographed, another Airstreamer came in just as mine were going into the dryer. As if we were on a schedule!

---

## Potluck & the Whitmans

For dinner, a potluck. Every evening before leaving a stop, Keith tells the group a little more about the Whitmans' journey on the Oregon Trail. It reminds me of a preacher — he holds the book open to a passage he's chosen and tells the tale between those pages.

![Potluck dinner with the Airstream caravan group](/images/day-9/IMG_4587.jpeg)

---

## The 1:12 Scale Chuckwagon

One of our couples brought a **1:12 scale chuckwagon** she built herself — she makes miniatures for dollhouses and said the wagon and its contents took about **300 hours** to complete. It was filled with miniatures of things the pioneers would have carried. It will be on display later in the trip, and I promise to get close-up pictures then. *(And get their names!)*
